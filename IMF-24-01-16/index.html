What is the initial mass function (IMF)?

Definition:

The initial mass function is the relative number of stars, as a function of their individual initial mass, that forms during a single star forming episode. [1]

$$ \small \xi(m) \equiv \frac{dN}{dm} $$

IMF is fundamental to getting a better understanding of

  • Star formation
  • Chemical enrichment
  • Stellar evolution
  • Stellar feedback
  • ...

Is IMF universal?


  • universal IMF: same IMF for different environments
  • No convincing evidence for non-universal IMF from the observations of the local Universe

[1]: Carigi, L. (2011)

Three most popular IMFs of the local Universe

  • Salpeter IMF: single power law [1]

    $$\xi(m) \propto m^{-2.35}, m \in [0.1, 100]~ M_{\odot}$$

  • Chabrier IMF: one power law + LogNorm in low mass end[2]

    $$ \begin{aligned} \xi(m) &\propto \frac{\ln 10}{m}\exp\left(-\frac{(\ln m - \ln 0.08)^{2}}{2\times 0.69^{2}} \right) , m\in (0.08,1]~M_{\odot} \\ \xi(m) &\propto m^{-2.3}, m > 1~ M_{\odot} \end{aligned} $$

  • Kroupa IMF: piecewise power law[3]

    $$\begin{aligned} & \xi(m) \propto m^{-0.3}, m \in(0,0.08]~M_{\odot} \\ & \xi(m) \propto m^{-1.3}, m \in(0.08,0.5)~M_{\odot} \\ & \xi(m) \propto m^{-2.3}, m \in[0.5,120]~ M_{\odot} \end{aligned}$$

[1]: Salpter (1955)

[2]: Chabrier (2003)

[3]: Kroupa (2001)

Why the intermediate- and high-mass regime?

Intermediate- and high-mass stars:

  • Mass range: $M\gtrsim 2 M_{\odot}$
  • Main sequence time $t_{\mathrm{MS}}\approx t_{\odot}(M/M_{\odot})^{-2.5}\lesssim 1.77~\mathrm{Gyr}$
  • IMF can be described by a simple power law

[1]: Karakas & Lattanzio (2014)

What are local red nugget analogs (LRNAs)?

  • Red Nuggets: massive, compact, quiescent, ellipticals, z~2
  • Local red nugget analogs: the local (z<0.1) analogs of red nuggets

[1]: NASA's Chandra X-ray Observatory

[2]: Yıldırım et al. (2017)

How does the LRNAs form?


  • The IMF is exactly the IMF of red nuggets.
  • The information of stars with $M\lesssim 2 M_{\odot}$ is contained in the hot gas.
  • X-ray observations matter!

Model selection criterion

We can fit the observations with different models, but which one is the best? We need AIC criterion. [1][2] $$ \small \mathrm{AIC}_{c} \equiv -2\ln \left( L^{0}(M) \right) + 2k + \frac{2k(k+1)}{N-k-1} = \mathrm{AIC}+\frac{2k(k+1)}{N-k-1} $$ The last two terms is penalty terms for the number of free parameters.
$\mathrm{AIC}_{c}$: the smaller, the better. $$ \small \Delta \mathrm{AIC}_{c} = \mathrm{AIC}_{c} - \mathrm{AIC}_{c,\mathrm{min}} $$
For C-statistic[3]: $$ \small C = 2\sum_{i}(M_{i}-D_{i}\ln M_{i}+D_{i}\ln D_{i} - D_{i}) $$ $$ \small -2\ln \left( L^{0}(M) \right) = C $$
$$\Delta \mathrm{AIC}$$ Level of Empirical Support
0-2 Substantial
4-7 Considerably less
>10 Essentially none

[1]: Akaike (1974)

Summary

  • IMF is one of the fundamental ingredient in galatic physics
  • X-ray observation is crucial for putting constraints on IMF in intermediate- and high-mass regime
  • The IMF of LRNAs is exactly the IMF of their progenitors at z~2
  • There is a hint of a steeper IMF in the intermediate- and high-mass regime
